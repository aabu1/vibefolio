## Design Context

### Users
Portfolio visitors — hiring managers, collaborators, product people, and fellow builders. They arrive from a link share or LinkedIn and need to quickly understand Ali's craft and what he builds. The portfolio serves as proof of work: real, interactive products they can use right there.

### Brand Personality
**Bold, ambitious, modern.** Forward-leaning confidence backed by shipping real products. Not quiet or understated — the work speaks loudly through its presentation.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone:** Premium minimal with confident presence — closer to Stripe's weight than Linear's lightness
- **References:** Vercel, Stripe, Linear (for structure and restraint, not for copying)
- **Anti-references:**
  - No generic dev portfolio patterns (GitHub contribution grids, tech logo walls, "Hi, I'm a developer" hero text)
  - No Dribbble/Behance showcase energy (flashy gradients, 3D renders, over-designed case study layouts)
  - No corporate SaaS landing page patterns (testimonial carousels, pricing tables, aggressive CTAs)
- **Theme:** Light mode (#FAFAFA background, white cards, monochromatic palette)
- **Accent:** Single indigo (#6366f1), used sparingly
- **Typography:** Inter — tight tracking for headlines, relaxed leading for body text

### Emotional Target
"This is impressive." — The presentation itself should signal high standards. Visitors should feel the craft in spacing, motion, and typography before they even interact with the embedded projects.

### Design Principles
1. **Ship over show** — Embed working products, not screenshots. The portfolio proves competence through live artifacts.
2. **Restraint is confidence** — Monochromatic palette, generous whitespace, no decoration that doesn't earn its place.
3. **Typography carries hierarchy** — Weight, size, and tracking do the heavy lifting. No color-coding or icons needed for structure.
4. **Motion with purpose** — Every animation should feel intentional (fade-in-up on load, hover lifts on cards). No ambient motion or loops.
5. **Precision signals quality** — Consistent spacing, aligned elements, and deliberate sizing. Details compound into impression.

### Tech Stack
- Next.js 16 (App Router), React 19, TypeScript
- Tailwind CSS 4 with CSS custom properties
- Inter via next/font
- No component library — pure Tailwind utilities

### Color Tokens

### Spacing Scale
- Base units: 4px, 8px, 12px, 16px, 24px, 32px, 48px, 64px
- Max content width: 1200px
- Horizontal page padding: 24px (px-6)

### Motion
- Entry: fade-in-up, 600ms ease-out, staggered 100ms per section
- Hover: shadow-lg + translateY(-4px), 300ms
- Transitions: 200-300ms for color/border changes

### Accessibility
- Standard best practices: good contrast, keyboard navigation, semantic HTML
- No special WCAG audit requirements
